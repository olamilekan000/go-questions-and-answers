# Making Decisions – Questions

## Question 1 – How are booleans and comparison operators used in Go?
Explain how boolean values are produced in Go and list the main comparison operators with examples.

## Question 2 – What logical operators does Go provide and how do they behave?
Describe the logical operators `&&`, `||`, and `!` in Go and how they combine boolean expressions.

## Question 3 – How do you write a basic `if`/`else` statement in Go?
Show the structure of a simple `if`/`else` in Go and explain why conditions must be boolean.

## Question 4 – What is short‑circuit evaluation and why is it important?
Define short‑circuiting in the context of Go’s logical operators, and describe how it affects function calls in compound conditions.

## Question 5 – How can you keep variable scope tight in `if` statements?
Explain the pattern of using an initialization statement in an `if` and why it’s useful for error handling.

## Question 6 – Why doesn’t Go have a ternary operator?
Discuss the trade‑offs behind Go’s lack of a `?:` ternary operator and how you would express similar logic.

## Question 7 – How is a `switch` statement structured in Go?
Describe the basic form of a `switch` in Go, including the role of `case` and `default`, and how control flow works.

## Question 8 – What is `fallthrough` in Go’s `switch`, and when would you use it?
Explain how `fallthrough` changes normal `switch` behavior and provide an example scenario where it can be helpful.

## Question 9 – How can a single `case` match multiple values?
Show how to write a `case` that matches more than one value and why this can simplify conditional logic.

## Question 10 – What does it mean to switch “without a condition”?
Describe a `switch` without an explicit tag expression and explain how this pattern can replace a ladder of `if`/`else` range checks.
