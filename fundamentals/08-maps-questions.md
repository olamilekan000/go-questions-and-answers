# Maps and Relationships – Questions

## Question 1 – What is a map in Go and when is it useful?
Explain what a map represents conceptually and give examples of when to choose a map over other data structures.

## Question 2 – How do you create and initialize maps?
Show how to create maps using `make` and map literals, including specifying key and value types.

## Question 3 – How do you check for the presence of a key?
Explain the “comma ok” idiom for maps and how it distinguishes missing keys from zero values.

## Question 4 – How do you delete keys and count elements?
Describe how to remove a key from a map and how to get the current number of key‑value pairs.

## Question 5 – How do you iterate over a map, and what should you know about order?
Explain how to loop through all keys and values in a map and why you cannot rely on iteration order.

## Question 6 – How can you retrieve all keys from a map?
Outline a pattern for extracting the set of keys into a slice for further processing.

## Question 7 – How do you sort map entries by key or by value?
Describe how to combine maps with slices and the `sort` package to produce ordered views of map data.

## Question 8 – How can maps and structs be combined?
Explain how to build maps of structs (and maps of pointers to structs) and why that can be useful.

## Question 9 – What are common pitfalls or limitations of maps in Go?
Discuss issues such as nil maps, concurrent access, and the lack of guaranteed ordering.

## Question 10 – How do maps relate to JSON and configuration data?
Describe when a `map[string]interface{}` or similar structure is appropriate for loosely structured data.
